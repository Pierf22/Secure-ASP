Uploading and Sharing Signed Files

In this tutorial, you'll learn how to securely upload your signed files to the server and share them with trusted recipients. Follow each step carefully to ensure secure handling and distribution of your files.

Adding Your Certificate

To start sharing files, you’ll first need to upload your certificate. Log in, navigate to Your Encodings, and click on the "Add Certificate" button.

Example of "Add Certificate" button in the Certification section

Adding Encoding Files

Once your certificate is uploaded, you can begin adding your signed encoding files. Follow these steps to securely upload and configure your encodings:

Phase 1: General Info

Note: If an encoding is set to public, anyone with a valid, server-signed certificate will be able to access it.

Start by adding basic information for your encoding file. Here, you can provide a name, description, and set the encoding to public or private, depending on your sharing preferences.

Example of General Info screen for encoding setup

Phase 2: Upload Files

Upload both the main encoding file and the signed version of the file, which was signed with your private key. This step ensures the authenticity and security of the uploaded file.

Example of Upload screen for the main and signed file

Phase 3: Team Access

Add team members with whom you wish to share the encoding. Each user will be able to use their own certificate to access the encoding, ensuring secure, certificate-based access for authorized users only.

Example of Team Access configuration screen

Phase 4: Add Tags

Tags allow you to categorize your encoding files, making it easier for others to understand the purpose of each encoding. Tags can help organize and describe your encodings more effectively.

Example of adding tags to organize encodings

Viewing and Editing Your Encoding

After uploading your encoding, you can view its details and adjust parameters as needed. Follow these steps to access and edit your encoding:

Phase 1: Viewing Your Encoding

Navigate to the encoding details page to view the encoding’s information, including its name, description, tags, and access settings.

Example of viewing encoding details

Phase 2: Editing Encoding Parameters

To modify your encoding, go to the settings section and adjust parameters such as the name, description, visibility, and tags. Once edited, save your changes to update the encoding settings.

Example of editing encoding parameters screen

Downloading the Encrypted File of Your Encoding

Once your encoding is set up and securely uploaded, you can download the encrypted version of the file. You can access the original file using your server-signed certificate, ensuring secure access to your signed encoding.

Example of encrypted file download screen
